How Does It Work?

We use a special device (Pwnagotchi) to test your Wi-Fi security. Here’s what happens:

  1. We temporarily disconnect a device from your network to trigger a "handshake"—a special moment when your device and Wi-Fi exchange security info.
  2. Our device captures this handshake and saves it as a file (a pcap).
  3. We try to decrypt the handshake. If we succeed, your Wi-Fi password is weak and needs attention. If not, your network is strong!

How Do We Know If Your Wi-Fi Is Secure?

We use a special device called a Pwnagotchi to test your Wi-Fi security. Here’s how it works:

  1. Handshake Capture: We temporarily disconnect a device from your Wi-Fi (with your permission). When it reconnects, your network and device exchange a special security packet called a "handshake." Our device captures this handshake file.
  2. Password Test: We try to decrypt the handshake using advanced tools. If we can crack the password, it means your Wi-Fi is not secure and needs a stronger password. If we can’t, your network is secure against common attacks!
  3. Clear Results: You get a simple report: Safe or Needs Attention. We’ll also give you tips to improve your security if needed.

We never keep or share your data. The test is quick, safe, and only checks your Wi-Fi password strength—nothing else!
